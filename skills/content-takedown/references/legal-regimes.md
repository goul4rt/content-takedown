# Legal regimes (verify fast-moving details at runtime)

## US — DMCA §512
- §512(c)(3) notice needs six elements: (1) signature of authorized person; (2) identification of the copyrighted work; (3) identification of the infringing material + location (URL); (4) contact info of the complaining party; (5) good-faith-belief statement; (6) accuracy + authority statement under penalty of perjury.
- §512(f): liability for knowing material misrepresentation (notice OR counter-notice).
- §512(g): counter-notice restores material in 10–14 business days unless the claimant files suit. Providers increasingly exercise discretion on whether to forward counter-notices.
- Designated-agent directory: copyright.gov/dmca-directory ($6 registration, 3-year renewal).
- Limit: a non-US host that never claimed §512 safe harbor owes no DMCA duty; use local law or the host AUP.

## US — TAKE IT DOWN Act (in force)
- Signed 19 May 2025. Criminal provisions (publishing/threatening NCII, incl. AI "digital forgeries" of real people) effective immediately.
- Platform notice-and-removal duties enforceable from 19 May 2026; FTC enforces (UDAP). Remove reported NCII + known identical copies within 48 hours of a valid request.
- Valid request: signature; info to locate content; good-faith non-consent statement; requester contact.
- Good-faith removal safe harbor. Report non-compliant platforms at TakeItDown.ftc.gov.

## US — NO FAKES Act (NOT law — watch)
- S.4591 advanced from Senate Judiciary 18 Jun 2026. Federal voice/likeness right; DMCA-style notice-and-takedown; platform liability on knowledge. Verify enactment before relying on it.

## EU — DSA
- Art. 16 notice-and-action: platforms must offer easy electronic notice mechanisms; name/email may be waived for CSAM notices.
- Art. 22 trusted flaggers: designated by national Digital Services Coordinators; notices get priority. Central list at transparency.dsa.ec.europa.eu (EU-based entities only). Verify current list.
- Enforcement/fines and delegated acts evolve — verify at runtime.

## EU — Right to be forgotten (GDPR Art. 17)
- Google Spain (C-131/12) established delisting; Google v CNIL (C-507/17, 2019) limits territorial scope to EU Member-State versions + geo-blocking, NOT worldwide — but a national DPA may still order global delisting in a specific case.

## France — LCEN / SREN
- LCEN Art. 6 base regime; loi SREN (2024) transposes/aligns with DSA. PHAROS reporting at internet-signalement.gouv.fr. Verify whether prior contact with the publisher is required.

## Germany — DDG
- NetzDG repealed May 2024; replaced by the Digitale-Dienste-Gesetz (DDG), which implements the DSA. DSC sits at the Bundesnetzagentur.

## UK — Online Safety Act
- Illegal-content duties enforceable since 17 Mar 2025; child-safety duties since 25 Jul 2025. Ofcom enforces (fines up to 10% of global turnover or £18m; can seek court blocking). Systemic regime (risk assessments + codes), not a per-item public takedown channel.

## Brazil — Marco Civil (Lei 12.965) after STF Temas 987 + 533 (26 Jun 2025)
Art. 19 declared partially and progressively unconstitutional (acórdão published 6 Nov 2025). Regimes:
- **Immediate removal on knowledge (grave-crime "rol taxativo", systemic-failure liability):** antidemocratic acts (CP arts. 286 sole para., 359-L/M/N/P/R); terrorism (Lei 13.260/2016); incitement/assistance to suicide or self-mutilation (CP art. 122); incitement to discrimination (race, colour, ethnicity, religion, national origin, sexuality, gender identity); homophobic/transphobic conduct (Lei 7.716/1989 arts. 20–20-C); crimes against women by reason of sex; sexual crimes against vulnerable persons / child pornography (CP arts. 217-A–218-C; ECA arts. 240, 241-A, 241-C, 241-D); human trafficking (CP art. 149-A). Isolated single items → art. 21 regime instead.
- **Court order still required:** crimes against honour (calúnia, injúria, difamação) — art. 19 preserved; voluntary removal by notice still allowed.
- **General rule (art. 21 notice-and-takedown):** other unlawful content and inauthentic-account complaints — liability after notification. Art. 21 (originally NCII) extended to crimes/unlawful acts generally.
- **Presumption of liability WITHOUT notice:** unlawful content in paid ads/boosting or bot/chatbot distribution networks; provider escapes only by proving diligent, timely action.
- **Replicas:** once content is judicially recognized as unlawful, all providers must remove identical posts on judicial OR extrajudicial notice.
- **Foreign platforms:** must maintain a Brazilian legal representative (juridical person) with full administrative/judicial powers.
- No strict liability (except the presumption cases). Electoral-law and TSE acts reserved.

## Brazil — other
- Lei 9.610/98 (copyright). ECA art. 241 (CSAM). SaferNet reporting channels for online crimes against minors.
- **ECA Digital (Lei 15.211/2025):** in force 17 Mar 2026; ANPD is the enforcing authority (Decreto 12.622/2025; regulation Decreto 12.880/2026). Platforms must detect/remove child sexual exploitation, grooming, cyberbullying and self-harm content; report to authorities; retain related data ≥6 months. Removal requestable by the victim, guardians, Ministério Público, or protection entities. Fines up to 10% of the group's Brazil revenue, capped at R$50m/infraction.
